## stablecoins development in the tradfi space

- Treasury and FinCEN/OFAC jointly proposed GENIUS Act implementing rules on April 8, requiring permitted payment stablecoin issuers (PPSIs) to establish AML/CFT programs and mandatory sanctions compliance — treating PPSIs as "financial institutions" under the Bank Secrecy Act. ([U.S. Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0435))
- The proposed rule grants states wide latitude to regulate sub-$10B issuers under state regimes "substantially similar" to the federal framework, creating a two-track licensing structure. ([ABA Banking Journal](https://bankingjournal.aba.com/2026/04/proposed-rule-would-give-states-wide-latitude-to-set-stablecoin-regulation/))
- White House CEA published an analysis finding that prohibiting stablecoin yield payments could materially reduce bank lending capacity — a key policy tradeoff embedded in the GENIUS Act debate. ([White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/research/2026/04/effects-of-stablecoin-yield-prohibition-on-bank-lending/))
- OCC's GENIUS Act proposal requires banks wishing to issue stablecoins to obtain a new "permitted payment stablecoin issuer" charter designation, per Morgan Lewis briefing for prospective issuers. ([Morgan Lewis](https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2026/04/occs-genius-act-proposal-what-prospective-issuers-need-to-know))
